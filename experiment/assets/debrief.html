Thank you for taking part in the study!

This study aimed to see if the inverse base-rate effect was present in this simple experiment, where errors could not be made. Using the previous work on the inverse base-rate effect (see Don, Worthy and Livesey (2021) for review), we hypothesise that when the ability to make errors in the learning phase is removed, participants will not exhibit the inverse base-rate effect.
