Viral Diagnosis

In this online study you will be asked to complete two phases: one learning phase and one testing phase. In the learning phase you will be shown statements about people having various symptoms that are linked to a disease, e.g., “Jonathan has a rash and a fever which means he has disease Z.” All diseases are completely fictional. After 16 trials you can choose to move onto the testing phase or you can complete all the learning trials before moving onto the testing phase.

In the testing phase you will be asked to identify which symptoms are linked to which diseases by pressing a specific key for each disease. The keys will correspond to the disease name. No feedback will be given to you in either phase.

The purpose of this experiment is to study  basic processes of learning. There are no foreseeable risks or discomfort associated with completing this study.

You are free to withdraw at any time during the study, any completed tasks until the point of leaving will be deleted and destroyed. You are also free to withdraw your data at any time up until the point of anonymous publication of these data, which will occur at the time the journal article is be published. To withdraw during the experiment, simply close your browser tab.

0.5 point will be allocated for taking part in the study.
